Background

What is HPV?

Human papillomavirus (HPV) is one of the most common sexually transmitted infections. It can be transmitted during vaginal, anal and even oral sex.

It appears that HPV is responsible for:
90% of anal and cervical cancers
70% of vaginal and vulvar cancers
60% of penile cancers

How to prevent HPV?

It is recommended that women get vaccinated. Women aged 21 - 65 years should get screenings. The use of condoms is important.

Lifetime risk of Cancers in South African Women:

Breast Cancer (1 in 27)

Cervical Cancer (1 in 42)

Colorectal Cancer (1 in 132)

Our Mission

Our mission is to reduce the infection rate of HPV by working with various communities and educating them on how to avoid infection, where to get screened, and the vaccination process. HPV can have severe long-term effects, but these issues are preventable. You can do your part by getting Screened, Vaccinated, and Using Protection.
