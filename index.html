Background

What is HPV?

Human papillomavirus (HPV) is one of the most common sexually transmitted infections.
It can be transmitted during vaginal, anal and even oral sex.

How to prevent HPV?

It recommended that women vaccinated.
Women ages 21 - 65 years old should to get screenings.
The use of condoms is important.

Lifetime risk of Cancers
in South African Women:

Breast Cancer (1 in 27)

Cervical cancer (1 in 42)

Colorectal Cancer (1 in 132)

Our Mission

It is our mission to reduce the infection rate of HPV as much as possible by working with various communities and educating them on how to avoid infection, where to get screened and the process of vacination. HPV can have severe effects in the long term, however these issues are preventable.
You can do your part by getting Screened, Vaccinated and Use Protection.
